On the Subject of Shorting Subcircuits

This circuit looks complicated, but maybe if I could break it down...

Presented before you is a KTANE_SS series logic board. Each KTANE_SS series board controls a fundamental part of the bomb, such as the timer, the strike controller, etc. To safely disarm the module, you must identify the subcircuits of the logic board and short the connections between them.

Identifying Subcircuits

A subcircuit is a set of integrated circuits (ICs) such that electricity can flow between every IC in the set, either directly or through another IC in the subcircuit. Note that electricity only flows along connections in one direction.

To identify which ICs electricity can flow to from a particular IC, hold the red multimeter probe on the "source" IC (where electricity flow originates). This will cause the LED of the "source" IC to flash and the LEDs of each connected "destination" IC (where electricity flow terminates) to light up. Remember that electricity only flows in the indicated direction (i.e., if electricity flows from IC 02 to IC 05, it does not flow from IC 05 to IC 02).

Example of Subcircuit Identification

For example, if electricity can flow from IC 01 to IC 02, from IC 02 to ICs 03 and 04, and from IC 03 to ICs 01 and 04, then ICs 01, 02, and 03 form a subcircuit. Since electricity cannot flow from IC 04 to ICs 01, 02, or 03, it is not part of the subcircuit.

Disarming the Module: Shorting Connections

To disarm the module, the connections between subcircuits must be shorted. This will involve shorting individual connections from ICs in one subcircuit to ICs in a different subcircuit.

Once you have identified each subcircuit present on the logic board, you must find the single subcircuit from which electricity cannot flow to another subcircuit. This will be called a terminal subcircuit. Any subcircuits from which electricity can flow to another subcircuit will be called internal subcircuits.

To short a connection between two ICs, place the red probe on the "source" IC in the internal subcircuit and place the black probe on the "destination" IC in the terminal subcircuit. The power supply will automatically short the connection after 2 seconds. Shorting any other connection or attempting to short a non-existant connection will result in a strike. To cancel a short, remove the black probe from the IC that it is touching.

Note well that to safely disarm the module, you may only short connections from internal subciruits to terminal subcircuits (i.e., you must short all connections which lead into terminal subcircuits). Shorting a connection may cause an interal subcircuit to become a terminal subcircuit. Once all connections between subcircuits are safely shorted using the guidelines listed above, the module will be disarmed.

Example of Safely Shorting Subcircuits

Consider three subcircuits which have been identified as follows:

  • Subcircuit A contains ICs 00 and 01
  • Subcircuit B contains ICs 02 and 03
  • Subcircuit C contains ICs 04 and 05

The following three inter-subcircuit connections have also been identified:

  • Connection from IC 00 to IC 02
  • Connection from IC 02 to IC 04
  • Connection from IC 03 to IC 05
  • Connection from IC 01 to IC 05

In this example, subcircuit C is a terminal subcircuit and subcircuits A and B are internal subcircuits. Thus, all connections terminating in subcircuit C must be shorted first: 02↦04, 03↦05, and 01↦05.

After this, subcircuit C is now disconnected and subcircuit B is now a terminal subcircuit. Thus, all connections terminating in subcircuit B must be shorted next: 01↦05.

After this, all connections between subcircuits will have been shorted and the module will disarm.
